Polynomial rings and polynomials

Let $R$ be a ring, $p = \sum_{i=0}^d p_i x^i \in R[x]$ a polynomial, $p_d \neq 0$.

CodeMathematics
Rx, x = PolynomialRing(R, "x")Returns the pair $(R[x], x))$
Rx([1, 2, 3])Returns the polynomial $3x^2 + 2x + 1$
base_ring(p)Returns $R$
map_coeffs(f, p)Returns the polynomial by applying $f$ to every coefficient of $p$
change_base_ring(S, p)Returns the polynomial by coercing every coefficient of $p$ to $S$
parent(f)Returns $R[x]$
gen(Rx)Returns $x \in R[x]$
coeff(p, i)Returns $p_i$
setcoeff!(p, i, c)Sets $p_i$ equal to $c$
degree(p)Returns $d = \operatorname{deg}(p)$
length(p)Returns $\operatorname{deg}(p) + 1$
trail(p)The coefficient of the terms with lowest degree
zero(Rx)Returns $0 \in R[x]$
one(Rx)Returns $1 \in R[x]$
iszero(p)Returns whether $p = 0$
isone(p)Returns whether $p = 1$
isgen(p)Returns whetehr $p = x$
isunit(p)Returns whether $p \in R[x]^\times$
subst(p, a)Returns $p(a)$ where $a$ can be anything
interpolate(Rx, s, t)Given $s, t \in R^n$ find the unique polynomial $q \in R[x]$ of degree $\leq n - 1$ such that $q(s_i) = t_i$
gcdinv(p, q)Returns $(\gcd(p, q), r)$ such that $pr \equiv 1 \pmod q$
resx(p, q)Returns $(\operatorname{res}(p, q), s, t)$ such that $\operatorname{res}(p, q) = st + pq$
discriminant(p)Returns the discriminant of $p$
resultant(p, q)Returns the resultant of $p$ and $q$
sylvester_matrix(p, q)Returns the Sylvester matrix of $p$ and $q$
derivative(p)Returns the derivative of p
integral(p)Returns the integral of p
gcd(p, q)Returns the gcd of $p$ and $q$
lcm(p, q)Returns the lcm of $p$ and $q$
content(p)Returns the content of $p$
primpart(p)Returns the primitive part of $p$
compose(p, q)Returns the composition $p(q(x))$
trunace(p, n)Remove all terms of degree $\geq n$ of $p$
mullow(p, q, n)Returns $pq \pmod{x^n}$
reverse(p)Returns $\sum_{i=0}^d p_{d - i} x^i$
shift_left(f, n)Returns $p \cdot x^n$
shift_right(p, n)Returns $p$ divided by $x^n$
mulmod(p, q, r)Returns $pq \pmod r$
powmod(p, n, r)Returns p^n \pmod r$
divides(p, q)Returns (true, $r$) with $pr = q$ if $p$ divides $q$ and (false, ?) otherwise
valuation(p, q)Returns the largest $n$ such that $q^n$ divides $p$
remove(p, q)Returns $(n, r)$ such that $p = q^n \cdot r$ and $q$ does not divide $r$
pseudorem(p, q)Returns the pseudoremainder of $p$ by $g$
pseudodivrem(p, q)Returns the pseudoquotient and pseudoremainder of $p$ by $g$
divrem(p, q)Returns the quotient and remainder obtained by the Euclidean division of $p$ by $q$
mod(p, q)Returns the remainder obtained by the Euclidean division of $p$ by $q$
rem(p, q)Returns the remainder obtained by the Euclidean division of $p$ by $q$
gcdx(p, q)Returns $(gcd(p, q), s, t)$ such that $\gcd(p, q) = sp + tq$
